L'Hôpital-Bernoulli Theorems

Theorem (L'Hôpital, J. Bernoulli) Suppose

  1. $I$ is a non-trivial real interval
  2. $a\in I$, (we can consider also $a \equiv -\infty$ and $a\equiv +\infty$
  3. $f:I\to\mathds R$ and $g:I\to\mathds R$ are two differentiable functions on $I$
  4. $\lim\limits_{x\to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$ exists (is a real number), denote it $L$,
then:
  1. the limit $\lim\limits_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$ exists (is a real number) and coincides with $L$
